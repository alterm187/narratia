# Narratia - Strategic & Tactical Plan
## Author Platform Development for Sebastian Proba

**Created:** November 2025  
**Planning Horizon:** 12 months  
**Core Philosophy:** Depth over frequency, sustainability over perfection

---

## Executive Summary

This plan recognizes your strengths (deep thinking, quality writing, bridging technology and spirituality) and constraints (introversion, limited social media energy, small current audience). The strategy prioritizes book sales and review acquisition over content marketing vanity metrics, with a sustainable approach that won't burn you out.

**Key Principle:** Your real work is writing books. Everything else supports that or supports book discovery.

## Part 7: Review & ARC Campaign Strategy

### Current Priority: Get to 50+ Reviews Per Book

**Why this matters:**
- Amazon algorithms favor books with more reviews
- Social proof for new readers
- Required for Bargain Booksy and similar promotions
- Builds credibility

### ARC (Advanced Review Copy) Campaign

**Setup:**

1. **ARC landing page on website**
   - "Join my ARC team"
   - What ARC readers get: free early copies
   - What you need: honest reviews
   - Clear expectations (read within 2-3 weeks, post review)
   - Signup form

2. **ARC reader agreement** (simple)
   - Free copy in exchange for honest review
   - Post on Amazon (and Goodreads if possible)
   - Timeline expectations
   - No obligation to give positive review

3. **Recruit ARC readers:**
   - Announce in newsletter
   - Post on social media
   - Ask existing readers
   - Use book blogger lists
   - Leverage your Substack audience

**ARC Process:**

1. Reader signs up
2. You send book (typically via BookFunnel or email)
3. Follow up before release date
4. Thank reviewers after they post
5. Share reviews on social media (with permission)

### Ongoing Review Generation

**Strategies:**

1. **Back matter in books:** Include page asking for review with specific Amazon link
2. **Email campaigns:** Gently ask subscribers who've had the book for 30+ days
3. **Social media asks:** Regular reminders that reviews help
4. **Personal requests:** Ask friends, family, beta readers directly
5. **Goodreads:** Build presence there, engage with readers
6. **Book blogger outreach:** Send copies to bloggers in your genre

**Target:**
- Get to 50+ reviews on each book within 6 months
- Then maintain with steady flow

## Part 11: Dealing with Low Engagement

### Mindset Shifts

**Reality checks:**
1. **Most authors start with tiny audiences.** Your current numbers are normal.
2. **Growth is exponential, not linear.** It feels slow until suddenly it doesn't.
3. **You're building assets.** Every post, every email, every page has compounding value.
4. **Quality readers > quantity.** 10 people who buy and review > 1000 passive followers.
5. **Your introversion is a feature, not a bug.** Depth attracts depth.

### What to Do When Motivation Drops

**Remember:**
- You're not doing this for likes; you're doing this so readers can find your books
- Social media vanity metrics don't equal book sales
- Every piece of content is a "search result" waiting to be found
- Your books are good; discovery takes time
- You're playing a long game

**Practical adjustments:**
- If a platform feels awful, do less there
- If something's working even a little, do more
- If you need a break, take it (but come back)
- Celebrate small wins (every new subscriber, every review)

**The permission slip:**
You don't have to be a "content creator." You're a novelist who occasionally shares thoughts. That's enough.

## Part 14: Emergency Protocols

### When You Feel Overwhelmed

**Minimum viable marketing (when life gets crazy):**

1. **Email list only:** Keep sending occasional updates
2. **Substack only:** One essay when you can
3. **Social media:** Let it go dormant if necessary (it's okay)
4. **Website:** Stays up, requires no maintenance
5. **Writing:** Always protect your creative time

**The nuclear option:** Everything can pause except:
- Your email list (email quarterly at minimum)
- Your books staying available for sale
- Your website staying live

### When Something Isn't Working

**Decision framework:**

**After 3 months:** If a strategy shows zero results, adjust or cut it
**After 6 months:** If still not working, probably cut it
**Exception:** SEO and content marketing take 6-12 months to show results

**Questions to ask:**
- Is this taking time away from writing?
- Is this draining my energy?
- Is there any positive indicator at all?
- Am I giving it a fair chance?
- What would I do instead?

## Part 18: Final Notes

### The Core Truth

You're a novelist exploring profound questions about consciousness, humanity, and spirituality in a technological age. That's rare and valuable. The work itself - the books - is what matters most. Everything in this plan exists to help readers discover your books and to build sustainable support for your writing life.

### Measuring What Matters

**Success is not:**
- Viral posts
- Huge follower counts
- Being "everywhere"
- Constant content production

**Success is:**
- Finishing your next book
- Readers finding and loving your current books
- A growing base of supporters who care
- Sustainable systems that don't burn you out
- Making progress, however slow

### The First Step

Don't try to do everything at once. Start here:

1. Set up email capture on your website
2. Create your Substack
3. Write one good essay
4. Batch one month of social posts
5. Then breathe and see what happens

### Remember

You already have the hard part done: you've written books worth reading. Now you're just letting people know they exist. This plan gives you a path, but you can modify it as you learn what works for you.

You've got this.
